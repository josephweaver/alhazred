# Session 001 - The Church Beneath the Rain

## Summary

Elias Thorne arrived at Saint Bartholomew Church seeking temporary shelter, food, and human contact during a cold rainstorm in the early collapse period.

Initial investigation revealed:
- recent occupation of the church,
- metallic resonance sounds beneath the sanctuary,
- a chained exterior cellar entrance,
- and evidence of active subterranean movement.

Inside the church Elias encountered Jonah Mercer, a survivor and caretaker who had established operational survival procedures regarding an unknown subterranean cognitive predator.

During the night:
- the first and second bells rang beneath the church,
- an entity ascended from below,
- Elias successfully avoided escalation through meditative cognitive suppression,
- and Mercer revealed that certainty, recognition, and conceptual fixation appear dangerous.

The night concluded peacefully.

Elias and Mercer established a cooperative survival pact and agreed to search the river district cannery warehouse together the following day.

---

# Major Discoveries

- The church contains an active subterranean entity.
- The entity appears responsive to cognition, recognition, and certainty.
- Questions appear safer than conclusions.
- Observational journaling may be safe if conclusions are avoided.
- The church bell system appears connected to escalation states.
- The creature may prefer isolated minds.

---

# Operational Procedures Learned

- Avoid prolonged visual fixation.
- Avoid completing mental patterns.
- Avoid naming or classifying the entity.
- Avoid panic within groups.
- Maintain cognitive flexibility.
- Controlled meditation and emotional regulation appear protective.

# Psychological Themes

This session reinforced several emerging survival principles:
