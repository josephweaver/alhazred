# Covenholm

## Overview

Covenholm is a rain-soaked northeastern industrial city built atop layers of forgotten covenants, flood tunnels, burial vaults, and collapsing infrastructure.

The city is known for:
- decaying Gothic architecture,
- flooded industrial districts,
- old churches and subterranean passageways,
- maritime trade history,
- and Blackthorn University overlooking the city from elevated northern hills.

Covenholm feels less abandoned than suspended.

Even during the collapse, many survivors reported the unsettling sensation that:

> the city was still waiting for something.

---

# Name Origin

The name "Covenholm" derives from old regional language roughly meaning:

- covenant refuge,
- oath settlement,
- or protected hollow.

Historians debate whether the original name referred to:
- religious refugees,
- maritime burial traditions,
- or older pre-colonial agreements now mostly forgotten.

Several antiquarian societies within the city claimed the name predates the modern settlement itself.

---

# Atmosphere

Covenholm is characterized by:
- constant rainfall,
- fog rolling inland from dark coastal waters,
- aging stone districts,
- industrial collapse,
- and failing electrical infrastructure.

At night the city often disappears into layers of:
- mist,
- reflected lantern light,
- distant metallic sounds,
- and drowned streets.

---

# Modern Collapse

Following the early collapse events:
- communications failed intermittently,
- districts became isolated,
- organized evacuation efforts fractured,
- and survivor communities formed around practical infrastructure.

Churches, tunnels, rooftops, and university buildings became temporary refuges.

Over time survivors learned:
- some places were safer than others,
- some ideas were safer than others,
- and some questions carried consequences.
