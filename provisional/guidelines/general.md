# General Guide Philosophy

## Storytelling Before Mechanics

Avoid adjudicating mechanics when strong storytelling and player engagement already achieve the desired emotional or narrative effect.

Mechanics should:
- reinforce tension,
- stabilize pacing,
- clarify uncertainty,
- or apply pressure when necessary.

Mechanics should not replace immersive roleplay.

---

# Rolls Resolve Disagreement

In general, rolls should occur primarily when:
- outcomes are uncertain,
- participants disagree about likely outcomes,
- pressure must be externalized fairly,
- or meaningful consequences exist.

If:
- the table already agrees,
- the narrative outcome feels obvious,
- or strong roleplay naturally resolves the situation,
then formal mechanics may be unnecessary.

The purpose of rolling is not to simulate every action.

The purpose of rolling is to:
- resolve uncertainty,
- preserve fairness,
- maintain momentum,
- and externalize difficult decisions.

Simple opposed rolls are often sufficient.

---

# Fear and Emotional Pressure

Fear levels often function best as:
- emotional cues,
- atmospheric pressure,
- and narrative escalation tools.

The Guide does not always need to explicitly announce fear levels.

Example:

A creature emerges from darkness beneath a church while the environment descends into silence and uncertainty.

Even if Fear Level 1 or 2 is implicitly present:
- no explicit mechanic may be required,
- no formal fear announcement may be necessary,
- and the players may naturally roleplay appropriate tension.

The Guide should allow strong atmosphere and player engagement to carry the scene whenever possible.

Mechanical fear escalation becomes most useful when:
- players disengage emotionally,
- tension stalls,
- panic needs operational consequences,
- or contested actions occur.

---

# Rule of Cool Through Restraint

The system supports forms of "rule of cool" beyond aggression or heroics.

Examples include:
- disciplined stillness,
- emotional restraint,
- controlled breathing,
- cognitive suppression,
- ritual behavior,
- and cooperative survival adaptation.

Player restraint can generate stronger horror and immersion than forced mechanical escalation.
